OneShot: The game you shouldn't know about.

By Firdaus
10 January 2025

OneShot is a wonderfully unique puzzle-adventure game that stands out for its heartfelt narrative and intriguing gameplay mechanics. Developed by Future Cat LLC, the game captures the player’s attention with its emotionally rich story and charming world.


In OneShot, you play as Niko, a young child who awakens in a mysterious, dying world. Tasked with restoring the sun to bring life back to this decaying land, Niko embarks on an adventure filled with puzzles, strange characters, and emotional moments. The central plot, centered around hope, sacrifice, and the weight of responsibility, draws players in as they guide Niko on their journey. The game has an understated elegance, where every little detail in the environment and character interactions feels purposeful, contributing to the greater narrative of the world’s fate.


The puzzles themselves are inventive, requiring thoughtful exploration and interaction with the environment. Some of the puzzles demand outside-the-box thinking, and it’s refreshing to see how the game incorporates non-traditional elements into its mechanics. The world is crafted with care, each area brimming with its own personality and a sense of mystery that beckons players to uncover its secrets.

Visually, OneShot uses a minimalist pixel art style that manages to convey warmth and depth, capturing a sense of nostalgia while still feeling fresh. The character designs are simple yet expressive, and the environments are beautifully rendered, creating an atmosphere that draws players in and makes them feel like they’re a part of the world. It’s hauntingly beautiful, with tracks that evoke both sadness and hope, heightening the emotional impact of the story.


The interactions between Niko and the other inhabitants of the world are heartfelt, and each character feels genuine in their own way. The dialogue is poignant, adding layers to the narrative as you move through the game. The pacing of the story is well-balanced, with moments of lightheartedness and humor breaking up the more somber tones, creating an experience that feels rich and complete.

So why shouldn't you know about it? Well simply said the game main attraction is not knowing anything about the game. Think of it as less of a game and more towards a story, you would be more interested in knowing about a story if you haven't experienced it yet. Trust me, you'll want to know less before start playing the game. Go on, experience the world of OneShot with your own eyes, and may you find your solstice. Give OneShot, a shot.
